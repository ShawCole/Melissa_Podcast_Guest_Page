Moving Your New Site to amansala.com

Where We Are Now

The Problem

  • Your current site and blogs live on Squarespace — slow and tedious to update
  • New pages you've built with Claude are scattered across separate Netlify subdomains
  • Every new landing page means another random URL instead of living on amansala.com
  • You've invested years of work and money building SEO authority on amansala.com

What We're Doing

  • Move everything — new pages, landing pages, and blog — under amansala.com
  • No more subdomains. One site, one domain, all your content in one place
  • Keep every SEO ranking you've earned. Google won't notice the switch.
  • Build and launch new pages in minutes instead of fighting Squarespace

Will We Lose Our Google Rankings?

No. Google does not care what platform hosts your site. It cares about your domain name, your URLs, and your content. As long as amansala.com still serves the same content at the same URLs, your rankings stay exactly where they are. In fact, the new site will load faster — which Google rewards.

Phase 1 — Low Risk

Point amansala.com to Your New Site

We point your domain to Netlify, where all the new pages you've already built go live instantly under amansala.com. Your blog stays online seamlessly through a behind-the-scenes proxy — visitors see no difference.

  1. All new pages go live on amansala.com — homepage, retreats, landing pages, everything you've already built with Claude. No more scattered subdomains.
  2. Blog stays online automatically — Netlify quietly fetches your Squarespace blog content behind the scenes. Visitors still see amansala.com/blog/... as usual.
  3. Future pages are instant — any new page you build goes live on amansala.com immediately. No new subdomain needed, ever.
What happens behind the scenes: We update one DNS setting to point amansala.com at Netlify. A proxy rule keeps your blog visible while it still lives on Squarespace:
/blog/*  →  Squarespace (invisible to visitors and Google)

“Do we need a new subdomain every time we build a landing page?”

No. Once amansala.com points to Netlify, every page lives under one roof. Want to add a Mother's Day landing page? It goes live at amansala.com/mothers-day. A new retreat page? amansala.com/signature-retreat. One site, one domain, unlimited pages.

Phase 2 — Blog Migration

Bring the Blog Off Squarespace

Once the main site is stable, we move all blog content onto Netlify too — so you never have to touch Squarespace again.

  1. Export all blog posts from Squarespace
  2. Convert to fast, static pages with the exact same URLs — /blog/best-time-to-visit-tulum stays /blog/best-time-to-visit-tulum
  3. Preserve all SEO metadata — titles, descriptions, image alt text, headings
  4. Generate a fresh sitemap and submit to Google Search Console
  5. Remove the Squarespace proxy — everything is now 100% on Netlify

“We've put a lot of work and money into our current domain's SEO. How do we know it's safe?”

Your SEO authority is tied to your domain (amansala.com), not to Squarespace. We're keeping the same domain, the same URLs, the same content, and the same metadata. Google's crawler fetches HTML from a URL — it doesn't check who's hosting it. This is the same process major brands use when they re-platform, and we monitor everything for 30 days after to confirm.

Phase 3 — Monitor

Watch & Verify for 30 Days

We keep a close eye on Google Search Console and your analytics to confirm everything transferred cleanly.

  1. Watch for crawl errors and indexing issues in Google Search Console
  2. Verify no broken links on any previously-ranked pages
  3. Monitor organic traffic — should remain stable or improve due to faster load times
