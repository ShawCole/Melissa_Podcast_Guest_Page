Website Migration Plan

Squarespace → Netlify — preserving your SEO rankings every step of the way

Can the Rankings Transfer to Netlify?

Yes, 100%. Google does not care about hosting platforms. It cares about your domain, your URL paths, your content, and your page speed. If we serve the same content at the same URLs on a new host, Google won't even notice the switch.

Phase 1 — Low Risk

New Site on Netlify, Blog Stays on Squarespace

We launch the new homepage, retreats, and booking pages on Netlify while your blog content continues to be served transparently from Squarespace. Visitors and Google see everything under amansala.com — no disruption.

  1. Point amansala.com DNS to Netlify for the new homepage, retreats, and booking pages
  2. Use Netlify proxy rewrites to serve /blog/* from Squarespace transparently — users and Google only see amansala.com/blog/...
  3. SEO protected: ~90% of blog-driven rankings are preserved immediately because those URLs haven't changed at all
How the proxy works: Netlify fetches blog pages from Squarespace behind the scenes using a rewrite rule
/blog/*  https://old-amansala.squarespace.com/blog/:splat  200
Visitors never see the Squarespace origin — it's invisible.
Phase 2 — Blog Migration

Migrate Blog Content to Static HTML

Once the main site is stable, we bring all blog content onto Netlify as fast-loading static pages with the exact same URLs and SEO metadata.

  1. Export blog posts from Squarespace (Settings → Import/Export → XML)
  2. Convert to static HTML with the exact same URL slugs — /blog/best-time-to-visit-tulum stays /blog/best-time-to-visit-tulum
  3. Preserve all meta tags, headings, image alt text, and structured data
  4. Generate sitemap.xml and robots.txt
  5. Deploy to Netlify, remove proxy rewrite rules
  6. Resubmit sitemap to Google Search Console
Phase 3 — Monitor

Watch & Verify for 30 Days

We monitor Google Search Console and analytics to make sure everything transferred cleanly.

  1. Watch Google Search Console for crawl errors and indexing drops
  2. Verify no 404s on previously-ranked URLs
  3. Monitor organic traffic in analytics — should remain stable or improve

Why This Works

•

Same Domain

amansala.com stays amansala.com. All backlink authority stays with you.

•

Same URLs

Every page keeps its exact path. Google's index doesn't need to relearn anything.

•

Same Content

Identical text, images, and metadata. Google sees no change in quality signals.

•

Faster Speed

Static sites on Netlify CDN load faster than Squarespace — a positive ranking signal.
